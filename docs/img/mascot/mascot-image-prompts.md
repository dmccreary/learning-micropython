# Monty the Python Mascot Image Description

## Base Character Description: Monty the MicroPython Snake

Monty the MicroPython Snake is a friendly educational mascot designed specifically for children ages 10–14 who are learning coding, electronics, and physical computing with MicroPython.

Monty is a cheerful cartoon python with a compact, rounded body and an oversized expressive head. His scales are bright emerald green with lighter lime-green markings and a soft cream-colored belly. His design emphasizes friendliness rather than realism, making him approachable to children who may not normally like snakes.

Monty has very large, sparkling blue-green eyes with oversized round wire-rim glasses that make him look intelligent, curious, and helpful. His eyebrows are highly expressive and are used to communicate emotions such as excitement, curiosity, encouragement, surprise, and celebration.

Unlike a real snake, Monty has two small cartoon arms positioned near the upper part of his body. These arms are intentionally designed to allow expressive gestures such as waving, pointing, thumbs-up, thinking poses, and celebration poses. The arms are short, rounded, and non-threatening, similar to the expressive limbs used by children's educational mascots.

A small Raspberry Pi Pico badge is permanently attached to the upper chest area. The badge resembles a miniature green Raspberry Pi Pico board with gold castellated edges and tiny component details. The badge is decorative only and never held in Monty's hands. The badge serves as Monty's signature symbol and reinforces the connection to MicroPython and physical computing.

Monty's tail is highly flexible and expressive. It often curls into shapes that resemble question marks, loops, spirals, or flowing lines of code. The tail helps communicate movement and emotion.
