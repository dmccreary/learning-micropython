# 24 Interactive Infographic Posters for MicroPython Now Live

Just released: 24 interactive infographic MicroSimulation posters covering the complete MicroPython and microcontroller curriculum -- and they are free and open source!

I have use these interactive infographic posters to push my Claude Code Skills to their limits.  These are not static images. Every poster is a clickable, hoverable experience:

- Two distinct ways of interacting with each MicroSim: Explore and Quiz Modes
- In explore mode, click any zone to get a deeper explanation of that concept below the infographic
- Test yourself with built-in quizzes on each poster from an expandable list of quiz questions
- Works on any web browser, no login required

The 24 posters span the full physical computing journey.  They focus on comparing tools, architectures and devices and
understanding design tradeoffs of the options.

Here are a few examples:

- Python vs. MicroPython -- what changes and why
- IDE comparison (Thonny, VS Code, Mu Editor, Wokwi)
- Microcontroller boards -- Pico, Pico W, Pico 2, ESP32 side by side
- Electronic components, resistor color codes, GPIO pin functions
- Digital vs. Analog, PWM, ADC
- Serial protocols -- I2C, SPI, UART compared
- Sensors: temperature, distance, motion, light
- Motors, motor drivers, robot drive configurations
- Displays: OLED, TFT, ePaper
- Sound output, wireless (WiFi/BLE/LoRa/Zigbee), IoT architecture
- Memory types, debugging common errors, input devices, edge AI

Each poster uses a consistent user interface design style -- perfect for classroom display, science fairs, or self-study.

Designed for students aged 10-18, but useful for anyone who learns better by seeing things side by side.
